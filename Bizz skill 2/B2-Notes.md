# TCS Wings Exam - Business Skills 2 Notes

## 1. Personal Mastery - Working with Agility

### What is Personal Mastery?
- Continuous improvement of skills, knowledge, and emotional intelligence
- Self-awareness and commitment to lifelong learning
- Understanding your strengths, weaknesses, values, and vision

### Key Components:
**Self-Awareness:**
- Understanding your emotions, triggers, and behavioral patterns
- Recognizing gaps between current and desired state
- Regular self-reflection and feedback seeking

**Personal Vision:**
- Clear picture of desired future and goals
- Alignment of actions with long-term objectives
- Purpose-driven approach to work and life

**Mental Models:**
- Examining and challenging assumptions
- Being open to different perspectives
- Continuous questioning of beliefs

### Working with Agility:

**What is Agility?**
- Ability to adapt quickly to changing circumstances
- Flexibility in approach while maintaining focus on goals
- Quick learning and unlearning capabilities

**Characteristics of Agile Professionals:**
- **Adaptability:** Comfortable with change and uncertainty
- **Quick Learning:** Rapidly acquiring new skills as needed
- **Resilience:** Bouncing back from setbacks
- **Proactivity:** Anticipating changes rather than reacting
- **Collaboration:** Working effectively in dynamic teams

**Practices for Agile Working:**
- Embrace iterative approach (test, learn, improve)
- Welcome feedback and act on it quickly
- Maintain growth mindset
- Break large tasks into smaller chunks
- Stay updated with industry trends
- Practice time-boxing for better focus
- Use retrospectives to improve continuously

**Benefits:**
- Better stress management
- Increased productivity
- Enhanced problem-solving
- Improved workplace relationships
- Career advancement

## 2. Complex Problem Solving Capabilities

### Understanding Complex Problems:
- Multiple interconnected variables
- No clear or single solution
- Require systems thinking
- Impact various stakeholders differently

### Problem-Solving Framework:

**Step 1: Problem Definition**
- Clearly articulate the problem
- Distinguish symptoms from root causes
- Ask "What exactly needs to be solved?"
- Use 5 Whys technique

**Step 2: Information Gathering**
- Collect relevant data and facts
- Identify stakeholders and their perspectives
- Research similar past problems
- Understand constraints and resources

**Step 3: Analysis**
- Break down the problem into components
- Identify patterns and relationships
- Use analytical tools (SWOT, Fishbone diagram, Pareto analysis)
- Consider multiple perspectives

**Step 4: Generate Solutions**
- Brainstorm multiple alternatives
- Think creatively and outside the box
- Don't judge ideas initially
- Consider short-term and long-term implications

**Step 5: Evaluate Options**
- Assess feasibility, cost, time, and impact
- Consider risks and benefits
- Use decision matrices
- Seek diverse opinions

**Step 6: Implementation**
- Create action plan with clear steps
- Assign responsibilities
- Set milestones and timelines
- Communicate clearly to all stakeholders

**Step 7: Monitor and Review**
- Track progress regularly
- Be ready to adjust approach
- Learn from outcomes
- Document lessons learned

### Essential Skills:

**Critical Thinking:**
- Questioning assumptions
- Evaluating evidence objectively
- Identifying biases
- Drawing logical conclusions

**Systems Thinking:**
- Understanding how parts affect the whole
- Recognizing feedback loops
- Seeing patterns and connections
- Anticipating unintended consequences

**Creative Thinking:**
- Generating novel ideas
- Making unusual connections
- Challenging conventional approaches
- Using techniques like SCAMPER, mind mapping

**Analytical Skills:**
- Data interpretation
- Pattern recognition
- Logical reasoning
- Quantitative analysis

### Tools and Techniques:
- Root Cause Analysis (5 Whys, Fishbone)
- SWOT Analysis
- Decision Trees
- Scenario Planning
- Cost-Benefit Analysis
- Stakeholder Analysis

## 3. Persuasion and Influencing Skills

### Understanding Persuasion:
- Art of convincing others to accept your viewpoint or take action
- Different from manipulation (which is unethical)
- Based on logic, emotion, and credibility

### Aristotle's Three Pillars:

**Ethos (Credibility):**
- Build trust and establish expertise
- Demonstrate integrity and reliability
- Show understanding of the subject
- Share relevant credentials and experience

**Pathos (Emotion):**
- Connect emotionally with audience
- Use stories and examples
- Appeal to values and beliefs
- Create empathy and shared understanding

**Logos (Logic):**
- Present facts and data
- Use logical arguments
- Provide clear reasoning
- Show cause and effect relationships

### Key Influencing Strategies:

**1. Reciprocity:**
- Give before you ask
- Offer help and value first
- Create sense of obligation naturally

**2. Consistency:**
- Get small commitments first
- Build on previous agreements
- Show how your request aligns with their past actions

**3. Social Proof:**
- Show that others are doing it
- Use testimonials and case studies
- Reference respected individuals or groups

**4. Authority:**
- Demonstrate expertise
- Use credentials appropriately
- Reference authoritative sources

**5. Liking:**
- Build rapport and relationships
- Find common ground
- Show genuine interest in others
- Use appropriate humor

**6. Scarcity:**
- Highlight uniqueness or limited availability
- Create sense of urgency (ethically)
- Emphasize what might be lost

### Communication Techniques:

**Active Listening:**
- Pay full attention
- Ask clarifying questions
- Paraphrase to confirm understanding
- Show genuine interest

**Framing:**
- Present information in positive or compelling ways
- Highlight benefits over features
- Use language that resonates with audience

**Storytelling:**
- Use narratives to make points memorable
- Include relatable characters and situations
- Create emotional connection
- Have clear beginning, middle, and end

**Body Language:**
- Maintain appropriate eye contact
- Use open postures
- Mirror subtly (build rapport)
- Be aware of cultural differences

**Asking Questions:**
- Use open-ended questions
- Guide thinking with strategic questions
- Show curiosity and respect
- Listen more than you speak

### Building Long-term Influence:

**Trust Building:**
- Keep commitments consistently
- Be transparent and honest
- Admit mistakes and learn
- Show reliability over time

**Expertise Development:**
- Continuously update knowledge
- Share insights generously
- Solve problems for others
- Be go-to person in your area

**Network Building:**
- Maintain diverse connections
- Help others without expecting immediate return
- Stay in touch regularly
- Be connector for others

### Ethical Considerations:
- Always be truthful and transparent
- Respect others' autonomy
- Don't manipulate or coerce
- Consider impact on all stakeholders
- Align persuasion with values
